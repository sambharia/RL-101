# RLHF Resources

Primary text is the RLHF Book (local source). External resources are chosen to be
high-trust and beginner-friendly for the foundations the book assumes.

## Knowledge

### Primary text
- **The RLHF Book — Nathan Lambert** (local: `../rlhf-book/book/chapters/`, web: https://rlhfbook.com)
  Our spine. Each lesson maps to one or more chapters. Use for: everything.
- **RLHF Book lecture videos — YouTube playlist**
  https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLL1tdVxB1CpVpEtMHxwuR4uI4Lxjw00_y
  Author-recorded lectures that mirror the book. Use for: hearing it explained aloud.

### Foundations (the prerequisites the book assumes)
- **3Blue1Brown — Neural Networks series** https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDNU6R1_67000Dx_ZCJB-3pi
  Best visual intuition for what a neural net is and how gradient descent works.
  Use for: Foundations lessons on NNs, gradients, backprop.
- **3Blue1Brown — Essence of Calculus** https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDMsr9K-rj53DwVRMYO3t5Yr
  Use for: refreshing derivatives/integration when gradients show up.
- **Andrej Karpathy — Neural Networks: Zero to Hero** https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLAqhIrjkxbuWI23v9cThsA9GvCAUhRvKZ
  Builds a language model from scratch in code. Use for: what a language model *really* is, tokens, next-token prediction.
- **OpenAI Spinning Up in Deep RL** https://spinningup.openai.com/
  The clearest free intro to RL fundamentals (policy, reward, return, policy gradients).
  Use for: RL foundations and the policy-gradient chapter.
- **Hugging Face — RL Course & TRL docs** https://huggingface.co/learn/deep-rl-course / https://huggingface.co/docs/trl
  Use for: practical, code-level grounding once concepts land.

## Wisdom (Communities)
- **Interconnects (Nathan Lambert's writing + Discord)** https://www.interconnects.ai/
  Author of the book; high-signal commentary on post-training. Use for: current debates, sanity checks.
- **r/reinforcementlearning** https://reddit.com/r/reinforcementlearning
  Use for: RL fundamentals questions, conceptual confusions.
- **r/MachineLearning** https://reddit.com/r/MachineLearning
  Use for: paper discussion, broader ML context.
- **EleutherAI / Hugging Face Discords**
  Use for: open-source LM training practitioners; real implementation wisdom.

### Probability & statistics (for the RL/RLHF math)
- **Seeing Theory — Brown University** https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/
  Beautiful interactive visualizations of probability distributions, expectation,
  and sampling (frequentist inference chapter). Use for: visual intuition on how
  sample averages converge to expectations. Best companion for Lesson 4+.
- **3Blue1Brown — Essence of Probability** (select videos from the main channel)
  https://www.youtube.com/c/3blue1brown
  Use for: visual intuition on Bayes, distributions, and the central limit theorem.
- **Stanford CS229 — Probability Review** (Autumn 2018 notes)
  https://cs229.stanford.edu/section/cs229-prob.pdf
  Concise 12-page review of distributions, expectation, variance, and Bayes' rule
  at the level assumed by ML courses. Use for: quick reference when equations appear.
