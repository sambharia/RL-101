# Established starting point and mission shape

Learner is an engineer (BITS Pilani, 99%ile JEE Mains) — strong reasoning and fast
uptake, but rusty math: solid on probability basics, has forgotten calculus/integration.
Can read/write basic Python, no ML libraries. New to AI/ML specifically.

The mission is **teaching-to-learn**, not research: the end goal is a clear "RLHF for
Beginners" blog series. This makes the teach-back (write the blog paragraph) the primary
assessment, with quizzes and spaced recall as support. Implication: lead with intuition
and mental models, refresh math just-in-time (especially gradients and integration when
policy gradients arrive), and judge mastery by whether they can explain it cleanly — not
by exposure. Time is explicitly not a constraint; mastery gates progression.
